About Me

I am a DAAD PhD Scholar in Planetary Science currently advancing my research at the Bayerisches Geoinstitut (BGI). I graduated from [Insert Previous University/Degree] before moving to Germany to pursue my doctoral studies. My work is deeply rooted in cosmochemistry, exploring the fundamental processes that shaped our early solar system.

Current Research

My research focuses on planetary differentiation processes and isotopic systematics. Specifically, I am analyzing various meteorite samples—including NWA 6901, Erg Atouila 001, NWA 11561, and NWA 12455—through petrographic reporting, mineral separation, and chronological analysis.

A significant portion of my lab work involves developing and refining methodologies for measuring Al-Mg isotope ratios in rock standards and meteorites to better understand the timelines of early solar system formation.

Selected Expertise
